Noetfield builds **bespoke deterministic brains for other builders' full stacks** — the same thing the founder built for himself: a raw deterministic core that holds *the client's* locked vocabulary/framing/strategy, calls soupy LLMs only as walled workers, runs their operation 24/7 without drift, and improves via gated definition-updates (never silent self-mutation). ## 1. The pain it sells against (why this is bigger than selling patterns)

Every serious builder using AI hits the founder's exact wall:
- **Drift** — the AI reinterprets their core terms; same question, different answers.
- **No coherence** — ten competent fragments (agents, prompts, docs) that don't share a spine; the puzzle stays incomplete even when every piece exists.
- **Can't trust it unattended** — a probabilistic core means hovering forever; no one can leave.
- **Grade-its-own-homework** — agents report green while things break.

The founder lived all of it. **"I had almost everything but the puzzle was still incomplete"** is the buyer's exact feeling. BaaS sells the missing piece: **coherence + determinism + trustworthy autonomy**, embodied as a brain that holds *their* meaning and doesn't drift.

This pain is universal among technical founders and teams → the market is much larger than buyers for any single pattern.

## 2. What the client gets (the deliverable)

A deterministic brain for their stack, built from the founder's own proven template:

1. **Locked definitions** — *their* vocabulary/framing/strategy/claims, extracted-then-approved (provenance-tagged, no blank-page), git-locked as their SSOT.
2. **A raw deterministic decision-core** — code that decides over their definitions (same state → same decision), on the kernel. NOT a model; empty + their meaning.
3. **A soup-wall** — LLMs (their choice, model-agnostic) called as disposable workers that draft/execute language-shaped subtasks; a deterministic sanitizer enforces they never introduce meaning/claims/status the core didn't decide.
4. **A gated learning loop** — failures → proposed definition-updates → sandbox → verify → client-approve → version bump. Their brain sharpens without drifting.
5. **The governance substrate** — independent verifier, gate, content-identity, receipts — so their brain is trustworthy unattended and everything it does is auditable.
6. **The embodied patterns** — zero-drift, anti-theater, auto-heal, poison-detection, running (see `pattern-factory.md`). These are the *features*.

**Delivered as:** their full-stack brain, running 24/7, that they can trust to operate without hovering — with receipts.

## 3. THE STAGED PROOF-GATE (the law that makes this honest — REVISED)

The gate is **staged, not binary.** What's gated is the *claim of proven autonomy*, not the *foundation work*.

**Sellable NOW (framed as foundation/installation, not proven autonomy):**
- Brain Audit / stack-coherence diagnosis
- Locked-definitions build (their SSOT, extract-then-approve)
- Brain v0 architecture: decision-spine, workflow map, agent boundaries, verifier/receipt loop, human-approval gates, first 3–5 gated automation loops
- Anti-theater verifier design, founder/operator load-reduction roadmap

**GATED until SourceA runs 24/7 with receipts:**
- "fully deterministic autonomous brain"
- "24/7 trustworthy unattended operation"
- "zero-drift proven across live operations"
- "managed brain that runs your full stack"

**Why this is honest, not overclaiming:** selling a *foundation build* is selling a real deliverable you can do today. Selling *proven autonomy* before SourceA proves it is theater. The line is: sell the **build**, not the **proven result**.

**The honesty-anchor per tier (the guardrail):**
- **Tier 1 (Audit)** needs *zero* proof — you diagnose *their* chaos; the deliverable is a report + roadmap. Nothing about your result is claimed.
- **Tier 2 (Install / Brain v0)** needs *at least one pattern proven on SourceA* (zero-drift) as the credibility anchor — so "I'll build your coherent brain" points to a real proof, even though the client's brain isn't running yet. Without that anchor, Tier 2 is theater one level down.
- **Tier 3 (Managed Brain)** needs SourceA running 24/7 with receipts — the original gate, correctly placed *here*, not on everything.

**Your own SourceA brain is the demo, case study, and template.** Building it is not a prerequisite to *all* selling — it's the credibility anchor that unlocks each tier in turn. Tier 1 sells before it; Tier 2 needs one proven pattern from it; Tier 3 needs it fully running. This *raises the stakes* on finishing your brain (each SourceA milestone unlocks a tier) without blocking all revenue now.
