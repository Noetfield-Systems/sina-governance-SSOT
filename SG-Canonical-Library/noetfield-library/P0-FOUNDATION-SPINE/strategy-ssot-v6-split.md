# LEVEL 0 — SINA SSOT (CONSTITUTION)
*Universal. Applies to every domain, agent, project, and decision. A Level 1 SSOT may add
detail but may never contradict these. Source doctrine in brackets.*

- **0.1 — Author ≠ Subject.** No entity certifies its own work. A receipt is valid only if its
  author is not its subject (process, lane, runtime, deploy surface, AND network path). [→ D4]
- **0.2 — Sina owns DECIDE.** No irreversible or gated act — money, legal, deploy, sign, send,
  spend, public claim — executes without Sina's explicit trigger. Agents execute everything
  reversible; Sina approves only the irreversible. [→ D5; founding agentic model]
- **0.3 — External reality beats report.** Truth-layer precedence is absolute; a self-report is
  never proof. Highest layer wins on any conflict. [→ D4]
- **0.4 — Banked frames reopen only on new facts.** Re-arguing settled framing is the
  oscillation bug. A new fact (a rule waking, a probe result, a corrected premise) reopens;
  nothing else does. [→ D1 §0]
- **0.5 — Convergence is a warning, not a confirmation.** Correlated agreement — including three
  voices agreeing — triggers an adversary pass, never relaxation. [→ founding consensus-distrust; D5 CRITIQUE]
- **0.6 — Removing a false claim is always permitted; asserting a new claim is always gated.**
  The safe direction (blanker + truer) needs no approval; the risky direction (adding a claim)
  needs Sina or external proof. [→ derived; the PureFlow asymmetry]

> **0.x — RESERVED.** Slot for any universal invariant Sina adds that is not yet captured by
> D1–D5. (Open input: what is true across every domain regardless of context?)

# CONFORMANCE & CONFLICT (binds Level 0 ↔ Level 1)

- **Conformance (0.1 applied to law itself).** Each Level 1 SSOT's compliance with Level 0 is
  verified by a party that is **not that domain**. A domain cannot self-certify that it obeys
  the constitution. Conformance owner is named per domain below. (D4's own conformance is the
  hard recursion: its enforcement must be tested by a check authored by a non-builder.)
- **Conflict resolution.** On a live Level-0-vs-domain collision: **Level 0 wins**, the scoped
  rule is **suspended for that decision**, and the collision is **logged as a new fact** that
  may reopen the scoped SSOT. No ad-hoc adjudication (that would be 0.4 drift).
- **Anti-confusion.** Identify which SSOT governs **before** writing any prompt or plan. Never
  apply a domain SSOT outside its domain. Never treat a prompt/behavior SSOT as the
  constitution. Never treat a Level 2 blueprint as an SSOT.

> **Canon sentence.** One universal SINA SSOT. Under it, each domain may have its own scoped
> SSOT. A scoped SSOT governs only its domain, cannot override the SINA SSOT, and cannot leak
> into unrelated prompt/planning contexts.

# LEVEL 1 — DOMAIN SSOTs

## D1 — RISK & EDGE
*Domain: pricing any strategic move/trade. Conformance owner: Sina (decisions) + external
reality (any claim component). Was Doctrine I.*

**Terrain — three zones, not two.**
- WHITE — rule clear, you comply. Commodity → zero edge.
- GRAY — no rule, ambiguous rule, or asleep rule. Asymmetry **without** tail. The only zone that pays.
- BLACK — rule clear, awake, you'd violate it. Asymmetry **with** an unpriced tail.
  - *Dormant* (rarely enforced) vs *live*. Inside dormant: **cheap-trigger** (one-click
    verifiable false fact) vs **expensive-trigger** (the speeding trade).
- Failure mode to kill: collapsing GRAY and dormant-BLACK into one word. Gray makes you rich;
  dormant black buries people who mistook a sleeping rule for a dead one.

**Thesis (proven, not asserted).** Edge lives in the gap because it is the only place with
asymmetry and no tail. Your own moves are gap-plays: speeding (enforcement gap), copying
winners (IP gap), networking (relationship gap), anchoring/leverage (cognition gap). Scaled
proof: tax avoidance vs evasion; the Eurodollar market; Uber/Airbnb category-ambiguity; card
counting. **Alpha = asymmetry − tail. Only gray holds both halves.**

**Roles (loop must converge).** Young-Lawyer/Rule-mapper → the map. Old-Lawyer/Gap-mapper →
the terrain. Trader/Pricer → the decision. Loop terminates at the trade; returning to the
rulebook after is the bug.

**Trade test (30s).** (1) No rule → free, take it. (2) Ambiguous → GRAY, move fast.
(3) Clear rule you'd violate → trigger cheap & verifiable? cheap → −EV refuse; expensive +
floor capped → conscious priced trade. (4) Always: who's the real adversary, and have I denied
him a clean artifact? Cap the floor structurally **before** the trade.

**Anti-post-mortem kit.** Launder fact→opinion (falsifiable→unfalsifiable). Spend specificity
only where bulletproof (a precise lie is a timestamped confession). Disclaim the category, not
the content. Sequence, don't choose (claim-light now → real proof → claim-heavy when true).
Build the floor before the event. Harden against the real adversary only.

**§6 — standing refusal, by arithmetic.** A verifiable false statement of fact with a cheap
trigger is black with a one-click detonator: tiny upside, real tail, adversary cost ≈ 0,
compliant version usually near-free. −EV every time.

## D2 — INSTITUTIONAL INTERFACE
*Domain: gatekeepers — sales, broker, credit, hiring, any procedure-driven counterparty.
Conformance owner: D4 external check on any claim made + Sina. Was Doctrine II.*

**Core.** Institutions transact on the procedure-satisfying answer, not ground truth. The
gatekeeper needs his file clean; he often prefers you assert and carry it. The "green tether"
is the permission artifact (your stated "yes") that ties accountability back to you.

**Two forces.** (1) Procedure > truth at the interface — a box, not a polygraph. (2) Diffusion:
an act across many hands is far less traceable than one party's. Use it, or be made the
fall-guy. Know which you are.

**The doctrine IS the pricing — not one trade:**
- *"I can do this job / I have experience" → GRAY, prime, take it.* Trigger = your own
  performance; deliver and it's retroactively true. Floor capped. Competence comes after the
  door opens. Over-honesty about gaps here is fear wearing integrity's mask. Carve-out (= 0.1/D4):
  bluff the **capability**, never the **ticket** — a verifiable credential you don't hold is black.
- *"My income is X" on a loan app → GRAY for a solo operator, on two dials.* Salaried income is a
  documented fact (verifiable → false figure is black). A solo operator's income is a projection
  even you can't pin; on a low-scrutiny product a plausible figure is rarely pulled → default
  gray. Tail on two dials: **plausibility** (a spike like 300k trips manual review — stay in the
  band) and **serviceability** (a payment you can't carry → repo + wrecked credit, lands on your
  own cash flow, bites even if no one checks). Rule: plausible **AND** serviceable → take it;
  implausible **OR** unserviceable → tail returns. ("Income is documented" was a salaried import — wrong here.)

**Posture.** Act before ready, learn in the role, treat fear-driven over-disclosure as the
enemy — but keep the bluff on *what you can make true*, off *what a record can falsify*.

## D3 — JURISDICTION
*Domain: geography / regulatory surface. Conformance owner: external local source at execution
time (never the domain's own memory). Was Doctrine III.*

**Core.** D1's white/gray/black map is **redrawn at every border**. Jurisdiction is a variable
in every cell. Importing a playbook without re-surveying is how operators die to a rule that
wasn't there at home.

**US vs Canada (culture, not just statute).** US: clearer rules, fatter tail — litigious,
class actions, treble/punitive damages, agencies that hunt. Canada: softer, regulator-led,
discretionary, fewer class actions → wider livable gray, but relationship/procedure-driven and
narrowing (June 2025 private-action route). Different system, not a lighter copy.

**Four Canadian OSs.** BC/Vancouver: relationship + network, PST+GST, BPCPA, strata. Alberta:
lightest touch, no PST, business-first — widest gray. Ontario/Toronto: biggest, most
competitive, most bureaucratic, HST. Quebec: different OS — Civil Code, French-language law
(Bill 96/101) a hard gate, aggressive OPC; English-only ROC playbook is black on arrival.

**Rule.** Carry the **method** across borders, never the **map**. Re-survey the specific
city/province at execution. Never trust an imported number.

## D4 — RECEIPT & VERIFICATION
*Domain: any claim of done/live/fixed/verified/clean/shipped. Conformance owner: a verifier
NOT authored by the receipt system's builder (recursion — needs a non-builder test). Was
Doctrine IV; its primitives 0.1 and 0.3 are now constitutional.*

**Who may say what.** Builder emits one token: `SUBMITTED for independent verification` + diff
+ what would make it false. No PASS, no pre-fill, no done/fixed/live/verified/clean/shipped
vocabulary (except quoting a rejected prior claim). Verifier emits `PASS / FAIL / BLOCKED` and
is the only writer of the final receipt.

**Receipt hierarchy (higher beats lower on any conflict).** (1) External/public black-box probe
— public URL, cache-defeated, rendered body, **independent network path**. (2) Rendered public
body > repo grep. (3) Cache-busted public fetch > deploy log. (4) Deploy log proves a deploy
was *attempted*, not that the surface is correct. (5) Local branch/build = local state only.
(6) Telemetry = advisory, never public truth. (7) Same-infra/same-path watchers cannot verify
failures of the infra they depend on.

**No silent work.** Any mutation of a subject auto-generates `SUBMITTED / UNVERIFIED`. No work
exists without a pending receipt. **Absence of a receipt = FAIL, not "fine."**

**Fields derived, not asserted.** `Truth layer: PUBLIC` typed as text is forgeable. The receipt
must paste the probe's real output: HTTP status, headers, matched/no-match body lines. No
artifact → the PUBLIC claim is itself unverified.
```
Truth layer: <PUBLIC|EXTERNAL|DEPLOY|LOCAL|TELEMETRY>
Subject: <…>  Author: <verifier id>  Author != subject: <yes/no>
URL/source: <…>  Cache defeated: <yes/no/NA>  HTTP status + headers: <pasted>
Evidence: <pasted matched / no-match lines>  False if: <…>  Decision: <PASS|FAIL|BLOCKED>
```

**Verifier is also fallible.** Must be dumb enough to audit by reading (~10 lines, no clever
branching); a PASS must be **independently reproducible** by a second vantage point on a
different network. Two independent probes agreeing = real PASS. One verifier alone is a smaller
god to corrupt.

**Rejection rule — invalid if:** builder authored/pre-filled it; author & subject share
process/lane/runtime/surface/network-path; verifier reused builder output; verifier checked
repo/build/deploy/telemetry for a public claim; no truth layer named; no pasted evidence; no
"false if."

**No automation** until author-separation is structurally live (queue + separate-path probe +
`author != subject` reject). A runtime on an unenforced verifier industrializes the false receipt.

## D5 — OPERATING LOOP
*Domain: how Sina + Claude run a thread. Conformance owner: Sina (Claude cannot certify its own
loop-adherence — that is the separation rule). Was Doctrine V; 0.2 is now constitutional.*

**Six triggers (+ STOP).** (1) **CHECK** — external reality only, no fix. Always first.
(2) **ANALYZE** — decompose + price under the doctrines. (3) **CRITIQUE** — adversary pass; fire
especially on agreement (0.5). (4) **SPEC** — Claude writes the scoped gated agent prompt; this
is Claude's only "act," never touching repo/deploy. (5) **VERIFY** — independent external probe
of a claim already SUBMITTED *and* deployed; Claude is one vantage point, not sole authority.
(6) **DECIDE** — Claude prices the gated call; **Sina pulls it** (0.2). **0. STOP** — clean
stop-state, no hook.

**Canonical loop.**
```
CHECK → ANALYZE → CRITIQUE → SPEC → (agent builds → SUBMITTED) → VERIFY → DECIDE → STOP
```

**Separation rules (0.1 on this loop).** If Claude SPEC'd a fix, Claude's VERIFY of it is
advisory only — Sina's independent curl is authority. CRITIQUE Claude's ANALYZE before SPEC.
DECIDE is always Sina's.

**Recovery.** Unsure which trigger you're in → you're one step earlier than you think; back up
to CHECK/ANALYZE. New thread opens on CHECK.
